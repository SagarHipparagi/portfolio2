About Me

I’m interested in how models learn from data, how assumptions and objective functions shape behavior, and why small design decisions often matter more than model size or complexity. Instead of treating ML systems as black boxes, I focus on understanding their internal logic, failure modes, and limitations.

My work emphasizes reasoning about model behavior, validating results with evidence, and building systems that can be tested and explained — not just optimized for metrics. I’m particularly drawn to problems where clarity and correctness matter more than novelty.
