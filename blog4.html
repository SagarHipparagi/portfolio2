ML Misconceptions

"Why Training Longer Didn't Help"

At first, I believed that if a model wasn't performing well, the solution was simple: train it longer.

But I learned that more training time does not always mean better learning.

In one project, the model improved early on, then stopped progressing. After that, continuing training didn't help â€” it made the model worse.

The reason was that the model had already captured the main patterns. Beyond that point, extra training started reinforcing noise and random correlations, especially because the dataset had some label inconsistencies.

I tried regularization and tuning, but those only helped slightly. The real issue wasn't training duration. It was data quality.

The lesson is clear: Training longer is often a response to uncertainty, not a real solution. Sometimes the correct fix is improving the dataset, not extending training.
